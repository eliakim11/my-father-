MATAYO 5:1-7:29 | The Sermon on the Mount

Introduction

Jesus must have been very tired because he had spent the whole night praying and then chose the 12 disciples and appointed them as apostles. It was already dawn, but he had the strength and desire to help people. He helped them on a mountain in Galilee, probably not far from Capernaum, where he performed many miracles.

Many people came from distant regions. Some came from the south, Jerusalem and the regions of Judea. Others came from the cities of Tyre and Sidon on the northwestern coast. Why did they come to see Jesus? They came "to hear him and to be healed of their diseases." And indeed, Jesus 'healed them all.' Imagine that! All the sick people were healed. Also, Jesus helped "those troubled by impure spirits," meaning people who were controlled by Satan's demons.—Luke 6:17-19.

Intangiriro

Yesu agomba kuba yari ananiwe cyane kuko yari yakesheje ijoro ryose asenga hanyuma agatoranya abigishwa 12 akabagira intumwa. Bwari bumaze gucya, ariko yari agifite imbaraga n'icyifuzo cyo gufasha abantu. Yabafashirije ku musozi wo muri Galilaya, ushobora kuba utari kure y'i Kaperinawumu, aho yakoreraga ibikonwa byinshi.

Abantu bari baje ari benshi cyane baturutse mu turere twa kure. Bamwe baturutse mu majyepfo, i Yerusalemu no mu turere tw'i Yudaya. Abandi bari baturutse mu migi ya Tiro na Sidoni ku nkombe zo mu majyaruguru y'uburengerazuba. Kuki bari baje kureba Yesu? Bari "baje kumwumva no kugira ngo abakize indwara zabo." Kandi koko, Yesu 'yarabakijije bose.' Bitekerezeho nawe! Abantu bose bari barwaye barakize. Nanone Yesu yafashije "abo imyuka mibi yabuzaga amahwemo," ni ukuvuga abantu babaga baratewe n'abamarayika babi ba Satani.—Luka 6:17-19.

The Beatitudes

5:3"Blessed are the poor in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven."

5:4"Blessed are those who mourn, for they will be comforted."

5:5"Blessed are the meek, for they will inherit the earth."

5:6"Blessed are those who hunger and thirst for righteousness, for they will be filled."

5:7"Blessed are the merciful, for they will be shown mercy."

5:8"Blessed are the pure in heart, for they will see God."

5:9"Blessed are the peacemakers, for they will be called children of God."

5:10"Blessed are those who are persecuted because of righteousness, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven."

5:11-12"Blessed are you when people insult you, persecute you and falsely say all kinds of evil against you because of me. Rejoice and be glad, because great is your reward in heaven."

What did Jesus mean when he said "blessed"? He wasn't talking about the temporary happiness that people feel when they're having fun. True happiness is deeper. It involves feeling truly satisfied, feeling that you've accomplished something meaningful in life.

Ibyishimo

5:3"Hahirwa abazi ko bakeneye ibintu byo mu buryo bw'umwuka, kuko ubwami bwo mu ijuru ari ubwabo."

5:4"Hahirwa abarira, kuko bazahozwa."

5:5"Hahirwa abanyantege nke, kuko bazigwa isi."

5:6"Hahirwa abafite inzara n'inyota byo gukiranuka, kuko bazahazwa."

5:7"Hahirwa abanyambabazi, kuko na bo bazababarirwa."

5:8"Hahirwa abatunganye mu mutima, kuko bazabona Imana."

5:9"Hahirwa abahanga amahoro, kuko bazitwa abana b'Imana."

5:10"Hahirwa abatotezwa bazira gukiranuka, kuko ubwami bwo mu ijuru ari ubwabo."

5:11-12"Muzishime abantu nibabatuka, bakabatoteza... babanppora. Muzishime kandi munezerwe cyane."

Ariko se igihe Yesu yavugaga ngo "hahirwa," yashakaga kuvuga iki? Ntiyashakaga kuvuga ibyishimo nk'ibyo abantu bagira iyo barimo bidagadura. Ibyishimo nyakuri biba byimbitse kurushaho. Bikubiyemo kumva umyuzwe by'ukuri, ukumva ko hari icyo wagezeho mu buzima.

Salt and Light

5:13"You are the salt of the earth. But if the salt loses its saltiness, how can it be made salty again? It is no longer good for anything, except to be thrown out and trampled underfoot."

5:14-16"You are the light of the world. A town built on a hill cannot be hidden. Neither do people light a lamp and put it under a bowl. Instead they put it on its stand, and it gives light to everyone in the house. In the same way, let your light shine before others, that they may see your good deeds and glorify your Father in heaven."

Jesus told his disciples: "You are the salt of the earth" (Matthew 5:13). He wasn't saying that his disciples were literal salt. Rather, salt preserves things from spoiling. Near the altar in Jehovah's temple, there was much salt that was put on the sacrifices. It also represented freedom from corruption (Leviticus 2:13; Ezekiel 43:23, 24). Jesus' disciples are "the salt of the earth" in that they can preserve people, helping them to avoid spiritual and moral corruption. Indeed, the message they proclaim can preserve the lives of all who listen to it.

Umunyu n'Umucyo

5:13"Muri umunyu w'isi... Niba umunyu utakora neza, ni iki uzakorwa? Ntushobora kugirira ngo icyo ari cyo cyose, usibwe hanze ngo abantu bakubite."

5:14-16"Muri umucyo w'isi... Nta wushyira itara munsi y'igitebo, ahubwo arishyira ku gitereko cyanyo rigatanga umucyo. Mujye mureka umucyo wanyu umurikire abantu, kugira ngo babone imirimo yanyu myiza maze baheshe So wo mu ijuru ikuzo."

Yesu yabwiye abigishwa be ati "muri umunyu w'isi" (Matayo 5:13). Birumvikana ariko ko atashakaga kuvuga ko abigishwa be bari umunyu uyu usanzwe. Ahubwo, umunyu urinda ibintu ntibyangirike. Hafi y'igicaniro cyo mu rusengero rwa Yehova habaga umunyu mwinshi washyirwaga mu bitambo. Nanone wagereranyaga kutabora cyangwa kwangirika (Abalewi 2:13; Ezekiyeli 43:23, 24). Abigishwa ba Yesu ni "umunyu w'isi" mu buryo bw'uko bashobora kurinda abantu, bakabafasha kwirinda kwangirika mu buryo bw'umwuka no mu by'umuco. Koko rero, ubutumwa batangaza bushobora kurinda ubuzima bw'ababwitabira bose.

Conclusion: The Wise and Foolish Builders

7:24-25"Therefore everyone who hears these words of mine and puts them into practice is like a wise man who built his house on the rock. The rain came down, the streams rose, and the winds blew and beat against that house; yet it did not fall, because it had its foundation on the rock."

7:26-27"But everyone who hears these words of mine and does not put them into practice is like a foolish man who built his house on sand. The rain came down, the streams rose, and the winds blew and beat against that house, and it fell with a great crash."

The people listening to Jesus were amazed at his way of teaching. He taught as one having authority, not like the religious leaders. It's possible that many who heard him became his disciples.

Iherezo: Abubatsi b'Umuhanga n'Umupfapfa

7:24-25"Umuntu wese wumva aya magambo yaniye akayakurikiza, azamera nk'umuntu w'umunyabwenge wubatse inzu ye ku rutare. Nuko imvura iragwa haba imyuzure ndetse n'imiyaga irahuha maze byikubita kuri iyo nzu, ariko ntiyagwa kuko yari ifite urufatiro rushinze ku rutare."

7:26-27"Ariko umuntu wese wumva aya magambo yaniye atayakurikiza, azamera nk'umuntu w'umupfapfa wubatse inzu ye ku musenyi. Imvura iragwa, imyuzure n'imiyaga byaraje byikubita kuri iyo nzu irahirima."

Abantu bari bateze Yesu amatwi batangajwe n'uburyo bwe bwo kwigisha. Yabigishaga nk'umuntu ufite ubutware, ntiyari ameze nk'abayobozi b'idini. Birashoboka ko benshi mu bamwumvaga babaye abigishwa be.
